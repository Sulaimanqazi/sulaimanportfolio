What learning style do I consider myself as?

I do see myself as a visual and kinesthetic learner, but not an auditory learner. For example, in my science, math, and English classes, I do not pay attention during the lessons, instead, I review the teacher’s lessons posted online and learn by myself. While I wish I was able to keep focus during these lessons, it’s very difficult to get myself to do so. This is why I do not see myself as an auditory learner. I do believe that I’m a visual learner. For example, whenever I solve a math problem, I like to draw it all out. It’s much easier for me this way. I also believe that I’m a kinesthetic learner. For example, I often create flashcards on “Quizlet” for me to study before tests. This has proven to be an effective method of memorization based on my previous results.

My thoughts on these results

MyBlueprint identifies me as primarily a visual learner. However, the results are somewhat even, where I am 41% Visual, 33% Auditory, and 26% Kinesthetic. I do mostly agree with these results. Even though I do not believe I am an auditory learner, I often make an effort to study in a way that aligns with auditory learning. For example, when I completely do not understand a math question I watch a Khan Academy video on it. I do not see myself learning specifically through one way only and I instead try different ways and see what works at that moment.

● Underline or highlight key ideas in your notes
This would be done to draw attention to anything important in notes
● Pause, and try to create a mental image in your mind, or pause, and draw a visual representation.
This makes us to mentally visualize the study material, allowing us to understand the material to a higher extent.
● Turn concepts into charts and diagrams
This allows us to use visual learning to understand and fully comprehend the concept.
